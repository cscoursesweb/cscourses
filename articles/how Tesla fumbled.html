Introduction

Tesla has been one of the most innovative companies in the world, revolutionizing the automotive industry with its electric cars and pushing the boundaries of technology with its Autopilot system. However, despite its successes, Tesla has also made some missteps that have cost it dearly. In this blog post, we'll take a look at some of Tesla's biggest blunders and how they could have been avoided.

The Model 3 Production Debacle

One of Tesla's biggest missteps was its handling of the Model 3 production process. The company had promised to produce 5,000 Model 3s per week by the end of 2017, but it failed to meet that goal. This caused a huge backlog in orders and led to customer frustration and delays in delivery times.

Tesla had set an ambitious goal for itself, but it failed to properly plan for how it would achieve that goal. The company underestimated the complexity of producing a mass-market electric car and didn't have enough resources or personnel to meet its target. It also didn't anticipate how long it would take to ramp up production or how difficult it would be to source parts from suppliers.

Tesla eventually managed to increase production levels, but not before suffering significant financial losses due to missed deadlines and customer dissatisfaction. To avoid similar issues in the future, Tesla needs to be more realistic about what it can achieve and plan accordingly for any potential delays or problems that may arise during production.

The Autopilot Controversy

Another major misstep by Tesla was its handling of Autopilot, its semi-autonomous driving system. The system has been involved in several high-profile accidents over the years, leading many people to question whether it is safe enough for use on public roads.

Tesla has been criticized for not doing enough to ensure that drivers are using Autopilot safely and responsibly. The company has also been accused of overselling Autopilot's capabilities by claiming that it can make cars "fully autonomous" when they are still far from being able to drive themselves without human intervention. This has led many people to believe that Autopilot is more advanced than it actually is, which could lead them into dangerous situations if they rely too heavily on the system while driving.

To address these issues, Tesla needs to be more transparent about what Autopilot can and cannot do and make sure drivers understand how they should use the system safely before allowing them access to it on public roads. The company should also invest more resources into developing safer autonomous driving systems so that drivers can trust them when using them on public roads.

The SolarCity Acquisition

Tesla's acquisition of SolarCity was another major misstep by the company as it ended up costing them billions of dollars in losses due to SolarCity's mounting debt burden and lack of profitability at the time of acquisition. This acquisition was seen as a bailout for Elon Musk's cousin Lyndon Rive who was CEO at SolarCity at the time as well as a way for Musk himself to benefit financially from his involvement with both companies since he owned shares in both companies prior to their merger.

This acquisition raised questions about whether Musk had acted ethically by using his position as CEO of Tesla to benefit himself financially while putting shareholders' money at risk with an unprofitable acquisition like SolarCity. To avoid similar issues in future acquisitions, Tesla needs better oversight from independent board members who can ensure that any acquisitions are done with shareholders' interests in mind rather than those of individual executives or board members who may have conflicts of interest due their involvement with other companies or individuals related with those companies being acquired by Tesla .

Conclusion

Tesla has made some major missteps over the years which have cost them dearly both financially and reputationally but these mistakes could have been avoided if proper planning had taken place beforehand or if better oversight had been put into place afterwards .
