Introduction

Algorithmic trading, also known as automated trading or algo-trading, is a type of trading that uses computer algorithms to automatically generate and execute trades. Algorithmic trading is used by many large institutional investors, such as hedge funds and investment banks, to quickly and efficiently execute large orders in the financial markets. Algorithmic trading can be used for a variety of different strategies, including market making, arbitrage, trend following, and portfolio optimization.

Algorithmic trading has become increasingly popular in recent years due to its ability to quickly and accurately execute trades. By using algorithms to analyze market data and make decisions about when to buy or sell securities, algorithmic traders can take advantage of opportunities in the markets that may not be available to manual traders. Additionally, algorithmic traders can often execute trades faster than manual traders due to their ability to quickly analyze data and make decisions.
