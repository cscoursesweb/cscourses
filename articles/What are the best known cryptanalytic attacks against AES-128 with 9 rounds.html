Cryptanalysis is the practice of breaking cryptographic algorithms and systems to gain access to encrypted data. AES-128 is a symmetric encryption algorithm that uses a 128-bit key to encrypt data. It is one of the most widely used encryption algorithms and is considered to be very secure. However, it is not immune to cryptanalytic attacks.

In the context of AES-128, the related-key attack works by finding two related keys that can be used to reduce the number of rounds required to break the encryption.


Finally, the most recent cryptanalytic attack against AES-128 with 9 rounds is the boomerang attack. This attack was first proposed by researchers in 2008 and is based on the concept of a boomerang.
