What is Compounding Interest?

Compounding interest is a type of interest that is calculated on both the principal amount of a loan as well as any accumulated interest from previous periods. This means that the more time passes, the more interest accumulates on top of the original loan amount. This type of interest can be beneficial for borrowers because it allows them to pay off their loans faster and save money in the long run.

How to Calculate Compounding Interest

Calculating compounding interest requires a few simple steps. First, you need to determine the principal amount of your loan, which is the initial amount borrowed before any additional fees or charges are added. Next, you need to determine your annual rate of return (APR), which is typically expressed as a percentage rate over 12 months. Finally, you need to calculate how many times per year your loan will compound (this is usually either monthly or quarterly). Once you have all these numbers in hand, you can use an online calculator or formula to calculate your total compounded interest rate over time.

Using Compounding Interest To Your Advantage

Once you understand how compounding works and have calculated your total compounded interest rate over time, there are several ways that you can use this information to your advantage. For example, if you know that your loan will compound monthly at an APR of 5%, then you can adjust your repayment schedule accordingly so that more money goes towards paying off principal each month instead of just accumulating more interest over time. Additionally, if you know that your loan will compound quarterly at an APR of 8%, then you may want to consider refinancing or taking out a new loan with a lower APR so that less money goes towards paying off principal each quarter instead of just accumulating more interest over time.
