How can I get notified when a device connects to my Wi-Fi hotspot on Google Pixel 6?

If you own a Google Pixel 6, you may be wondering how you can get notified when a device connects to your Wi-Fi hotspot. Fortunately, Google has made it easy to stay informed about who is connecting to your Wi-Fi hotspot. In this article, we’ll explain how to get notified when a device connects to your Wi-Fi hotspot on Google Pixel 6.


The first step is to open the Settings app on your Google Pixel 6. Once you’ve opened the Settings app, tap on the “Network & Internet” option. From there, tap on the “Wi-Fi” option. You should now see a list of available Wi-Fi networks. Tap on the network that you want to get notifications for when a device connects.


Once you’ve tapped on the network, you should see a list of options. Scroll down until you find the “Notifications” option. Tap on the “Notifications” option and then toggle the switch to the “On” position. This will enable notifications for when a device connects to your Wi-Fi hotspot.


Now that you’ve enabled notifications, you should receive a notification whenever a device connects to your Wi-Fi hotspot. The notification will include the device’s name, IP address, and MAC address. This information can be useful if you want to keep track of who is connecting to your Wi-Fi hotspot.


If you ever want to disable notifications for when a device connects to your Wi-Fi hotspot, simply follow the same steps as above and toggle the switch to the “Off” position. This will disable notifications for when a device connects to your Wi-Fi hotspot.
