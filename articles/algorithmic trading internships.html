Introduction

Algorithmic trading, also known as automated trading or algo-trading, is a type of trading that uses computer algorithms to automatically generate trading signals and execute trades. Algorithmic trading is used by many professional traders and financial institutions to make decisions about when to buy and sell stocks, bonds, commodities, currencies, and other financial instruments. Algorithmic trading can be used for both long-term investments and short-term trades.

Algorithmic trading is based on the use of mathematical models and algorithms to identify patterns in the market data. These models are then used to generate buy and sell signals that are sent to the trader’s computer or mobile device. The trader then decides whether or not to act on the signal. Algorithmic trading can be used for both manual and automated execution of trades.
