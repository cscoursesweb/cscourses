Educational Requirements

The first step in becoming a stock broker is to obtain the necessary education. Most employers require that applicants have at least a bachelor’s degree in finance or economics. However, some employers may accept applicants with degrees in other fields such as accounting or business administration. Additionally, many employers prefer applicants who have completed coursework in securities analysis and investments.

In addition to obtaining a degree, aspiring stock brokers must also complete certain courses related to the securities industry. These courses are typically offered by universities or professional organizations such as the Financial Industry Regulatory Authority (FINRA). The courses cover topics such as investment strategies, financial markets, and securities regulations. Completing these courses can help prepare you for the licensing exams required for becoming a stock broker.

Licensing Exams

Once you have obtained the necessary education and completed the required courses, you must pass two licensing exams administered by FINRA: Series 7 and Series 63 (or 66). The Series 7 exam covers topics such as stocks, bonds, options, mutual funds, taxation of investments, retirement plans, margin accounts, and more. The Series 63 exam covers topics related to state securities laws and regulations. Passing both of these exams is required for becoming a registered representative of any brokerage firm in the United States.

Job Outlook

The job outlook for stock brokers is positive due to an increasing demand for financial services from individuals and businesses alike. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS), employment of securities agents is projected to grow 6 percent from 2019-2029—faster than average for all occupations—with an estimated 11,400 new jobs created during this period. Additionally, BLS data shows that the median annual wage for securities agents was $64,120 in May 2019—higher than average compared with other occupations in finance and insurance industries.
