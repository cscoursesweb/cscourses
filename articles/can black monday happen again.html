Introduction

The stock market crash of 1987, known as Black Monday, was one of the most devastating financial events in history. On October 19th, 1987, the Dow Jones Industrial Average (DJIA) plummeted 22.6%, the largest single-day percentage drop ever recorded. The crash wiped out billions of dollars in wealth and sent shockwaves through the global economy. In the aftermath of this event, many people have asked: could it happen again?

What Caused Black Monday?

The causes of Black Monday are still debated to this day. Some experts point to a combination of factors such as high levels of debt, overvaluation of stocks, and a lack of liquidity in the markets. Others suggest that computerized trading algorithms were to blame for exacerbating the sell-off. Whatever the cause, it is clear that a perfect storm of conditions led to one of the worst days in stock market history.
