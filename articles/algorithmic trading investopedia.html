Introduction

Algorithmic trading, also known as automated trading or algo-trading, is the use of computer programs to execute trades automatically according to predetermined criteria. Algorithmic trading is used by institutional investors such as hedge funds and large investment banks, as well as individual traders. It has become increasingly popular in recent years due to its ability to reduce transaction costs and improve execution speed.

Algorithmic trading is based on the use of algorithms, which are sets of instructions that a computer program follows in order to make decisions about when and how to execute trades. These algorithms can be programmed to take into account a variety of factors such as market conditions, price movements, and other data points. The goal of algorithmic trading is to reduce the amount of time it takes for a trade to be executed and increase the accuracy of the trade execution.

Algorithmic trading can be used for a variety of different strategies including market making, arbitrage, trend following, and portfolio optimization. Market makers use algorithms to provide liquidity in the markets by continuously buying and selling securities at prices that are close to the current market price. Arbitrageurs use algorithms to take advantage of price discrepancies between different markets or exchanges. Trend followers use algorithms to identify trends in the markets and then follow those trends by buying or selling securities accordingly. Finally, portfolio optimization algorithms are used by investors who want to maximize their returns while minimizing their risk exposure.

Benefits of Algorithmic Trading

Algorithmic trading offers several advantages over traditional manual trading methods. First, it allows traders to execute trades quickly and accurately without having to manually enter orders into a broker’s system or wait for a human trader’s response time. This can be especially beneficial during times when markets are volatile or moving quickly since algorithmic traders can react more quickly than humans can.

Second, algorithmic trading reduces transaction costs since orders are executed automatically without any human intervention required. This means that traders don’t have to pay commissions or other fees associated with manual order entry which can add up over time if you’re an active trader.

Third, algorithmic trading allows traders to backtest their strategies before they go live with them in the markets which helps them identify potential problems before they start risking real money in the markets. This helps traders refine their strategies so that they have a better chance at success when they do start live trading with real money on the line.

Finally, algorithmic trading allows traders access to more data than they would have access too if they were manually entering orders into a broker’s system which helps them make better informed decisions about when and how much capital they should allocate towards each trade idea they have identified in the markets.
