Who Is Github'S Ceo?

GitHub is one of the most popular and widely used version control systems in the world. It is used by millions of developers and organizations to store and manage their code. But who is the CEO of GitHub?


The current CEO of GitHub is Nat Friedman. He was appointed to the position in October 2018, after Microsoft acquired GitHub for $7.5 billion. Prior to joining GitHub, Nat Friedman was the CEO of Xamarin, a mobile app development platform that was acquired by Microsoft in 2016.


Nat Friedman has a long history in the software industry. He was a co-founder of Ximian, a Linux desktop company, and was also a founding member of the GNOME Foundation. He has also held various positions at Novell, Microsoft, and Xamarin.


Nat Friedman is a strong advocate for open source software and has been a vocal supporter of the open source community. He has spoken at numerous conferences and events about the importance of open source and the need for collaboration between developers and organizations.


In addition to his role as CEO of GitHub, Nat Friedman is also a board member of the Open Source Initiative (OSI). The OSI is a non-profit organization that promotes open source software and works to ensure that open source software is available to everyone.
