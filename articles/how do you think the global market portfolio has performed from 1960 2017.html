Introduction

The global market portfolio is a collection of stocks, bonds, and other investments that represent the entire world's financial markets. It is a way to measure the performance of the global economy over time. In this blog post, we will take a look at how the global market portfolio has performed from 1960 to 2017.

The Global Market Portfolio in 1960

In 1960, the global market portfolio was made up of stocks and bonds from around the world. The majority of these investments were in developed countries such as the United States, Japan, and Germany. At this time, there were few emerging markets and most of these investments were concentrated in developed countries.

The performance of the global market portfolio in 1960 was largely driven by economic growth in developed countries. The United States was experiencing strong economic growth due to increased consumer spending and investment in new technologies such as computers and telecommunications. This led to strong stock market returns for investors in developed countries.

The Global Market Portfolio in 1980

By 1980, the global market portfolio had become more diversified with investments from emerging markets such as China and India beginning to make up a larger portion of the portfolio. This diversification helped reduce risk for investors as it spread out their investments across different regions and industries.

The performance of the global market portfolio during this period was largely driven by economic growth in emerging markets such as China and India which experienced rapid economic growth during this period due to increased investment from foreign companies looking to capitalize on these new markets. This led to strong stock market returns for investors who had invested in these emerging markets during this period.

The Global Market Portfolio in 2000

By 2000, the global market portfolio had become even more diversified with investments from all around the world making up a larger portion of it than ever before. This diversification helped reduce risk for investors as it spread out their investments across different regions and industries even further than before.

The performance of the global market portfolio during this period was largely driven by economic growth in both developed and emerging markets which experienced strong economic growth due to increased investment from foreign companies looking to capitalize on these new markets as well as technological advances such as computers and telecommunications which allowed businesses to operate more efficiently than ever before. This led to strong stock market returns for investors who had invested globally during this period.

The Global Market Portfolio Today

Today, the global market portfolio is even more diversified than ever before with investments from all around the world making up a large portion of it. This diversification helps reduce risk for investors as it spreads out their investments across different regions and industries even further than before while also providing access to new opportunities that may not be available locally or regionally.

The performance of the global market portfolio today is largely driven by economic growth in both developed and emerging markets which are experiencing strong economic growth due to increased investment from foreign companies looking to capitalize on these new markets as well as technological advances such as artificial intelligence which are allowing businesses to operate more efficiently than ever before while also providing access to new opportunities that may not have been available previously due to geographical or technological limitations.
