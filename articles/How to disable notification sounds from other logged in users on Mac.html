How to disable notification sounds from other logged in users on Mac?

If you share a Mac with other people, you may find yourself getting annoyed by the notification sounds coming from their accounts. Fortunately, there is a way to disable notification sounds from other logged in users on Mac. Here’s how to do it.


First, open System Preferences. You can do this by clicking the Apple icon in the top left corner of your screen and selecting System Preferences.


Once you’re in System Preferences, click on the Notifications icon. This will open up the Notifications pane.


In the Notifications pane, you’ll see a list of all the applications that can send you notifications. To disable notification sounds from other logged in users, you’ll need to uncheck the box next to each application. This will prevent the application from sending you notifications.


You can also customize the way notifications are displayed for each application. To do this, click on the application in the list and then click the Options button. This will open up a window where you can customize the way notifications are displayed.


Once you’ve finished customizing the way notifications are displayed, click the Done button. This will save your changes and prevent the application from sending you notifications.


You can also disable notifications from other logged in users by going to the Users & Groups pane in System Preferences. In the Users & Groups pane, you’ll see a list of all the users on your Mac. To disable notifications from a user, simply uncheck the box next to their name.


Finally, you can also disable notifications from other logged in users by going to the Notifications pane in System Preferences and clicking the Do Not Disturb button. This will prevent all notifications from being sent to your Mac.
