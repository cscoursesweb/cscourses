Why is there apparently a tradition of "flat-filing" file archives?

The tradition of “flat-filing” file archives is a practice that has been around for centuries. It is a method of organizing and storing documents and other records in a way that makes them easy to access and use. Flat-filing is a simple and efficient way to store and organize documents, and it is still used today in many different industries.


Flat-filing is a system of organizing documents in which each document is placed in a separate folder or envelope. The folders are then arranged in a flat file cabinet, usually in alphabetical order. This system makes it easy to find documents quickly and efficiently. It also allows for easy retrieval of documents when needed.


Flat-filing is a great way to organize documents because it is simple and efficient. It is also a great way to store documents for long-term use. Documents stored in flat-file cabinets are less likely to be damaged or lost than documents stored in other types of filing systems.


Flat-filing is also a great way to store documents for archiving purposes. Documents stored in flat-file cabinets can be easily accessed and used for research or other purposes. This makes it easy to find documents that may be needed for a project or research paper.


Flat-filing is also a great way to store documents for legal purposes. Documents stored in flat-file cabinets can be easily accessed and used for legal proceedings. This makes it easy to find documents that may be needed for a court case or other legal proceedings.


Flat-filing is also a great way to store documents for tax purposes. Documents stored in flat-file cabinets can be easily accessed and used for tax purposes. This makes it easy to find documents that may be needed for filing taxes or other tax-related purposes.
