How can i get colored text in macOS Ventura?

If you’re looking to add a bit of color to your text in macOS Ventura, you’ve come to the right place. Adding color to your text can help make your documents stand out and make them easier to read. Fortunately, macOS Ventura makes it easy to add color to your text with just a few simple steps.


The first step to getting colored text in macOS Ventura is to open the document you want to edit. Once you’ve opened the document, you’ll need to select the text you want to color. To do this, simply click and drag your mouse over the text you want to color. Once you’ve selected the text, you’ll need to open the Fonts window. To do this, simply click on the “Format” menu at the top of the screen and select “Fonts”.


Once you’ve opened the Fonts window, you’ll need to select the “Color” tab. This tab will allow you to choose the color you want to use for your text. You can choose from a variety of colors, including red, blue, green, and more. Once you’ve chosen the color you want to use, simply click “OK” to apply the color to your text.
