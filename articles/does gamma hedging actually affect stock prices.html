What is Gamma Hedging?

Gamma hedging is a strategy used by traders and investors to reduce the risk of their portfolios. It involves buying or selling options in order to offset the risk of a large move in the underlying stock. The goal of gamma hedging is to reduce volatility and limit losses if the underlying asset moves against your position.

The most common way to gamma hedge is by buying or selling options with delta values that are close to zero. Delta measures how much an option's price will change when the underlying asset moves one point up or down. Options with delta values close to zero are said to be "delta neutral" because they won't move much when the underlying asset moves one point up or down. By buying or selling these delta neutral options, traders can effectively hedge their positions against large moves in either direction.

When traders use gamma hedging, they are essentially creating an artificial demand for certain types of options contracts. This artificial demand can have an effect on stock prices, as it increases the demand for certain stocks and puts upward pressure on their prices. On the other hand, if a trader sells a call option with a delta value close to zero, they are essentially creating an artificial supply for that particular stock, which could cause its price to fall slightly due to decreased demand from traders using gamma hedging strategies.

Is Gamma Hedging Effective?

Gamma hedging can be effective at reducing volatility and limiting losses if the underlying asset moves against your position. However, it's important to remember that it's not foolproof and there is no guarantee that it will always work as intended.
