The most notable cryptocurrency crash was that of Bitcoin in 2018. Why Did Cryptocurrency Crash?

There are several factors that contributed to the crash of cryptocurrency prices in 2018. The first is speculation and market manipulation by large investors who were looking to cash out their profits before the market crashed. This caused a sudden influx of sell orders which drove down prices significantly.

Another factor was regulatory uncertainty surrounding cryptocurrencies at the time which caused investors to become wary of investing in them due to fears of government intervention or bans on trading them altogether. Finally, there was also a lack of liquidity in the markets which made it difficult for buyers and sellers to find each other and complete transactions quickly enough to prevent prices from dropping further.
