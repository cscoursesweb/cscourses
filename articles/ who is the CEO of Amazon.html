Introduction

Jeff Bezos is the founder and CEO of Amazon, one of the world’s largest online retailers. He is also the world’s richest man, with a net worth estimated at over $150 billion. Bezos has been at the helm of Amazon since its inception in 1994 and has been credited with transforming it into one of the most successful companies in history.

Bezos was born in Albuquerque, New Mexico in 1964. He attended Princeton University, where he graduated summa cum laude with a degree in electrical engineering and computer science. After college, he worked on Wall Street for several years before founding Amazon in 1994. Since then, he has grown it into a global powerhouse that now employs over 800,000 people worldwide and generates over $280 billion in annual revenue.

Bezos is known for his innovative approach to business and his commitment to customer service. He has been credited with pioneering new technologies such as cloud computing and artificial intelligence (AI). He also created Amazon Prime, which offers customers free two-day shipping on millions of items. In addition to his business acumen, Bezos is also an active philanthropist who has donated billions of dollars to charitable causes around the world.

The Early Years: How Jeff Bezos Founded Amazon

Before founding Amazon, Jeff Bezos had a successful career on Wall Street as a senior vice president at D.E Shaw & Co., an investment firm based in New York City. During this time, he developed an interest in technology and began exploring ways to use the internet to create new businesses.

In 1994, Bezos quit his job at D.E Shaw & Co., moved to Seattle and founded Amazon out of his garage. At first, it was just an online bookstore but soon expanded into other areas such as music and video streaming services as well as consumer electronics like Kindle e-readers and Fire tablets.

Bezos was determined to make Amazon stand out from other online retailers by offering customers superior service and convenience through features like free two-day shipping on millions of items through its Prime membership program. This strategy proved successful and helped propel Amazon’s growth into one of the largest companies in the world today.
