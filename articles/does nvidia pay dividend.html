What is a Dividend?

A dividend is a payment made by a company to its shareholders out of its profits or reserves. It is usually paid as cash, but can also be paid as stock or other property. Dividends are typically paid on a quarterly basis, although some companies may pay them more or less frequently.

There are several reasons why companies like Nvidia don't pay dividends.
