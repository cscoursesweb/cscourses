What exactly is a "pass" when talking about hashing, ciphers and MAC algorithms?

A “pass” is a term used to describe a key used in cryptography, which is the practice of using codes and ciphers to protect data. A pass is a secret piece of information that is used to encrypt and decrypt data, and it is typically a string of characters that is used to authenticate a user or to verify the integrity of a message.
