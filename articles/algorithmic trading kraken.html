What Is Algorithmic Trading?

Algorithmic trading is a form of automated trading that uses computer algorithms to determine when and how to trade financial instruments. It is a type of high-frequency trading, which is the use of sophisticated technological tools and computer algorithms to rapidly trade securities. Algorithmic traders use complex mathematical models and formulas to identify profitable opportunities in the markets. These models are programmed into computers which then execute trades automatically based on the parameters set by the trader.

Algorithmic traders can take advantage of market inefficiencies by executing trades faster than human traders can react. This allows them to capitalize on price discrepancies before they disappear or become too small for manual traders to take advantage of them. Additionally, algorithmic traders can backtest their strategies against historical data before deploying them in live markets. This helps them identify potential risks associated with their strategies before they start using them in real-time markets.
