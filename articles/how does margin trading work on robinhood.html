Introduction

Margin trading is a type of investing that allows investors to borrow money from a broker in order to purchase securities. This type of trading is often used by experienced traders who are looking to increase their potential returns. On Robinhood, margin trading is available for eligible customers who have been approved for margin trading privileges.

When you open a margin account with Robinhood, you will be able to borrow up to 50% of the purchase price of certain securities. This means that if you want to buy $1,000 worth of stock, you can borrow up to $500 from Robinhood and use it as part of your purchase. The amount that you can borrow will depend on your account balance and the type of security that you are buying.

In addition to being able to borrow money from Robinhood, margin accounts also allow investors to use leverage when trading stocks and other securities.
