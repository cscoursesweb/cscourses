What Is Return Smoothing?

Return smoothing is a technique used by investors to reduce volatility and smooth out returns over time. It involves taking profits from one investment and reinvesting them into another investment with similar characteristics but lower risk. This helps to reduce volatility in the portfolio, as well as reducing the overall risk associated with investing in private equity. The goal of return smoothing is to create a more consistent return profile over time, which makes it easier for investors to replicate private equity investments without taking on too much risk.

How Does Return Smoothing Work?

Return smoothing works by taking profits from one investment and reinvesting them into another investment with similar characteristics but lower risk. For example, if an investor has made a profit from investing in stocks, they could take those profits and reinvest them into bonds or other fixed-income securities with lower risk but similar characteristics.
