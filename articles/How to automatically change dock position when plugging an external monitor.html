How to automatically change dock position when plugging an external monitor?

If you’re a Mac user, you know how convenient it is to have a dock that’s always visible and accessible. But when you plug in an external monitor, the dock can get in the way and be a nuisance. Fortunately, there’s a way to automatically change the dock position when plugging in an external monitor. Here’s how to do it.


First, open System Preferences and select Displays. Then, click on the Arrangement tab. You’ll see a checkbox labeled “Mirror Displays”. Uncheck this box. This will allow you to move the dock to the external monitor.


Next, click on the Display tab. You’ll see a drop-down menu labeled “Position”. Select the external monitor from the drop-down menu. This will move the dock to the external monitor.


Finally, click on the Dock tab. You’ll see a checkbox labeled “Automatically hide and show the dock”. Check this box. This will ensure that the dock will automatically hide and show itself when you plug in or unplug the external monitor.
