AMD

AMD (Advanced Micro Devices) is one of NVIDIA's biggest competitors. They have been around since 1969 and have been a major player in the computer graphics and gaming industry for many years. AMD produces both GPUs (graphics processing units) and CPUs (central processing units). Their GPUs are used in many popular gaming consoles such as the Xbox One, PlayStation 4, and Nintendo Switch. AMD also produces CPUs for desktop computers, laptops, and servers. They offer competitive pricing on their products, making them a viable alternative to NVIDIA for many consumers.

They produce both CPUs and GPUs for desktop computers, laptops, servers, and gaming consoles. Intel's GPUs are used in many popular gaming consoles such as the Xbox One X, PlayStation 4 Pro, Nintendo Switch Lite, etc. Intel also offers competitive pricing on their products which makes them a viable alternative to NVIDIA for many consumers.

Qualcomm also offers competitive pricing on their products which makes them a viable alternative to NVIDIA for many consumers looking for mobile solutions or low-cost alternatives to traditional desktop/laptop solutions.
