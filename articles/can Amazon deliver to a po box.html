What Is a PO Box?

A PO box (or post office box) is an address provided by the United States Postal Service (USPS) for receiving mail and packages. It’s essentially a mailbox located in your local post office that you can use as your mailing address instead of your home address. This can be useful if you don’t want your mail delivered directly to your home or if you don’t have a physical address (such as in rural areas).
