Launching Mac Apps via iOS Shortcuts App


The iOS Shortcuts app is a powerful tool that allows you to automate tasks on your iPhone or iPad. It can be used to launch Mac apps, as well as other tasks such as sending messages, creating reminders, and more. In this article, we’ll show you how to launch Mac apps via iOS Shortcuts app.


Before you can launch Mac apps via iOS Shortcuts app, you’ll need to make sure that your Mac and iOS device are connected to the same Wi-Fi network. Once that’s done, you’ll need to enable the “Remote Login” feature on your Mac. To do this, open the System Preferences app on your Mac, click on the “Sharing” icon, and then check the box next to “Remote Login”.


Once you’ve enabled the “Remote Login” feature, you’ll need to open the iOS Shortcuts app on your iPhone or iPad. Tap the “+” icon in the top-right corner of the screen to create a new shortcut. Give your shortcut a name, and then tap the “Add Action” button.


In the search bar, type “Launch App” and select the “Launch App” action from the list. You’ll then be prompted to enter the name of the Mac app you want to launch. Type in the name of the app, and then tap the “Done” button.


Now that you’ve created your shortcut, you can launch Mac apps via iOS Shortcuts app. To do this, simply tap the shortcut you created, and the Mac app will launch on your Mac.
