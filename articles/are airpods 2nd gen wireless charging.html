How Does Wireless Charging Work?

Wireless charging works by using an inductive charger, which is a device that uses electromagnetic induction to transfer energy from one device to another without any physical contact. The inductive charger creates an electromagnetic field between two coils, one in the charger and one in the device being charged. When these two coils are close enough together, they create an electric current that charges the device.

The wireless charging case for the 2nd gen AirPods uses this same technology to charge your AirPods without having to plug them into a power source. All you have to do is place your case on top of a compatible Qi-certified wireless charger and it will start charging your AirPods automatically.

What Are The Benefits Of Wireless Charging?

Wireless charging offers several benefits over traditional wired charging methods. For starters, it’s much more convenient since you don’t have to worry about plugging in your device or dealing with tangled cords. It also eliminates wear and tear on your device’s ports since there’s no need for physical contact between the charger and your device.

Another benefit of wireless charging is that it’s faster than traditional wired methods since it can transfer more energy at once than wired chargers can handle. This means that you can get more power in less time when using a wireless charger, which makes it ideal for quickly topping off your battery when you’re on-the-go or need a quick boost of power before heading out for the day.
