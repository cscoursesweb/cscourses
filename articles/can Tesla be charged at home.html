What You Need to Charge a Tesla at Home

In order to charge your Tesla at home, you'll need a few things. First, you'll need an electric vehicle supply equipment (EVSE) charger. This is the device that will connect your Tesla to your home's electrical system and provide power for charging. You can purchase an EVSE charger from Tesla or from other retailers.

Next, you'll need an adapter that will allow you to plug the EVSE charger into your home's electrical system. Depending on where you live, this adapter may be included with the EVSE charger or may need to be purchased separately. Finally, if your home doesn't have a dedicated circuit for charging your Tesla, you'll need to have one installed by an electrician.

How Long Does It Take To Charge a Tesla at Home?

The amount of time it takes to charge a Tesla at home depends on several factors including the size of the battery pack in your vehicle and the type of charger being used. Generally speaking, it takes about 8-10 hours to fully charge a standard 75 kWh battery pack using an 11 kW Level 2 charger (the most common type). If you're using a higher-powered Level 3 charger (also known as Superchargers), charging times can be significantly reduced - as little as 30 minutes for some models!

How Much Does It Cost To Charge a Tesla at Home?

The cost of charging your Tesla at home will depend on several factors including electricity rates in your area and how much electricity your vehicle consumes while charging. Generally speaking, it costs about $0.12-$0.20 per kWh of electricity used for charging - so if you're using an 11 kW Level 2 charger with a 75 kWh battery pack, it would cost around $9-$15 per full charge cycle depending on local electricity rates.
