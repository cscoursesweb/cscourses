Is RSA longer supported in TLS 1.3 and are RSA and DH fundamentally different?

RSA and Diffie-Hellman (DH) are two of the most commonly used cryptographic algorithms for secure communication over the internet. Both algorithms are used to establish secure connections between two parties, but they are fundamentally different in how they work.


RSA is an asymmetric encryption algorithm that uses two different keys, a public key and a private key. The public key is used to encrypt data, while the private key is used to decrypt it. RSA is widely used in TLS (Transport Layer Security) protocols, including TLS 1.2 and earlier versions.


Diffie-Hellman (DH) is an algorithm for exchanging cryptographic keys over an insecure channel. It is a symmetric encryption algorithm, meaning that the same key is used to both encrypt and decrypt data. DH is used in TLS 1.3 and later versions, replacing RSA as the preferred algorithm for establishing secure connections.
