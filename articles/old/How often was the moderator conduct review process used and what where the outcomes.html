How often was the moderator conduct review process used and what where the outcomes?

The Moderator Conduct Review Process (MCRP) is a process used by organizations to evaluate the performance of moderators and ensure that they are adhering to the organization’s standards of conduct. The MCRP is used to assess the performance of moderators in a variety of areas, including their ability to maintain a safe and respectful environment, their ability to enforce rules and regulations, and their ability to respond to user complaints.


The frequency of the MCRP depends on the organization and the type of moderation being conducted. For example, some organizations may require moderators to undergo an MCRP every six months, while others may require it to be conducted annually. Additionally, some organizations may require moderators to undergo an MCRP after a certain number of user complaints have been received.


The outcomes of the MCRP vary depending on the organization and the results of the review. Generally, the outcomes of the MCRP can range from a warning to the moderator to a suspension or even termination of the moderator’s position. Additionally, the MCRP may result in the organization implementing additional training or resources for the moderator to ensure that they are better able to meet the organization’s standards of conduct.
