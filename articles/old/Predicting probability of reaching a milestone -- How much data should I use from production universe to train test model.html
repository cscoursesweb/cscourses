Predicting probability of reaching a milestone -- How much data should I use from production universe to train/test model?

Predicting the probability of reaching a milestone is an important task for any business. It can help inform decisions about how to allocate resources, plan for future growth, and identify areas of potential risk. But how much data should you use from the production universe to train and test your model?


The answer to this question depends on the type of data you are using and the complexity of the model you are building. Generally speaking, the more data you have, the better. However, it is important to consider the quality of the data as well as the quantity. If you have a large amount of data but it is of poor quality, it may not be useful for training and testing your model.


When it comes to the amount of data you should use, it is important to consider the size of the production universe. If the production universe is large, you may need to use a larger sample size to ensure that your model is accurate. On the other hand, if the production universe is small, you may be able to get away with using a smaller sample size.


It is also important to consider the complexity of the model you are building. If the model is complex, you may need to use more data to ensure that it is accurate. On the other hand, if the model is relatively simple, you may be able to get away with using a smaller sample size.


Finally, it is important to consider the type of data you are using. If you are using structured data, such as customer data or financial data, you may need to use a larger sample size to ensure accuracy. On the other hand, if you are using unstructured data, such as text or images, you may be able to get away with using a smaller sample size.
