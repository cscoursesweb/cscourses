In RSA, how to calculate the private exponent 'd', after choosing 'e'?

RSA is a public-key cryptography algorithm that is widely used for secure data transmission. It is based on the difficulty of factoring large numbers. In RSA, two keys are generated: a public key and a private key. The public key is used to encrypt data, while the private key is used to decrypt data.


In RSA, the public key consists of two numbers: the modulus 'n' and the encryption exponent 'e'. The private key consists of two numbers: the modulus 'n' and the decryption exponent 'd'. The modulus 'n' is the same for both the public and private keys.

Once the encryption exponent 'e' is chosen, the decryption exponent 'd' can be calculated. This is done using the Extended Euclidean Algorithm. This algorithm takes the modulus 'n' and the encryption exponent 'e' as inputs and calculates the decryption exponent 'd'.


The Extended Euclidean Algorithm works by finding the greatest common divisor (GCD) of 'n' and 'e'. The GCD is then used to calculate the decryption exponent 'd'. The decryption exponent 'd' is the inverse of the encryption exponent 'e' modulo 'n'.
