Two Sigma Securities: Combining Asset Management and Market Making

Two Sigma Securities is a company that combines both asset management and market making. The hedge fund side of the business has over $50B under management, while the market making entity regularly trades over 300 million shares per day in the US stock market.

Jump Trading: A Large Electronic Trading Firm with Focus on Futures and Crypto

Jump Trading was founded in 1999 by two former pit traders, Paul Gurinas and Bill Disomma. It is a large electronic trading firm that has grown to over 500 employees globally. The firm has significant market share in futures markets and has also expanded into cryptocurrency trading. Jump Trading is known for its quantitative and high-frequency trading strategies. The company has a strong technology focus and employs a team of developers to build custom trading systems.

Virtu Financial: A Leader in HFT with a Global Presence

Virtu Financial is a prominent player in the HFT space.

Founded in 2008 by Vincent Viola and Doug Cifu, the company is one of the largest high-frequency market makers globally.

Virtu has made some high-profile acquisitions of other trading firms and service providers including KCG Holdings and ITG.

The company has a particularly large presence in U.S. equities but also operates in other regions globally.

With a reputation for advanced technology and sophisticated trading strategies, Virtu Financial is a force to be reckoned with in the HFT landscape.

Citadel Securities: The Market Making Arm of Citadel LLC

Citadel Securities is the market making arm of Citadel LLC. The financial institution was founded by Ken Griffin in 1990 and manages over $30 billion in assets across multiple investment strategies. Citadel Securities accounts for over 10% of U.S. equity trading volume. It was formed in 2002, and its dominance in the market has grown significantly since then.

Hudson River Trading: A Quantitative Trading Firm Active in U.S. Stock Market

Hudson River Trading is a quantitative trading firm headquartered in New York City, founded in 2002. According to a Wall Street Journal article, the company is responsible for about 5% of U.S. stock market volume. The firm acquired competitor Sun Trading in 2017.
